## Short Project Description

Worthly is a local-first iOS prototype designed to help people understand where their money lives.

The app maps accounts, e-wallets, investments, liabilities, and transactions into one clearer view. Instead of starting as a full budgeting or accounting tool, Worthly focuses on visibility: helping users see their current net worth, where their assets are spread, what liabilities reduce the total, and whether their current position is moving toward a target.

## Medium Project Description

Worthly is a local-first iOS prototype built around a simple question: where is my money, actually?

The project started from the feeling of having assets spread across multiple places: bank accounts, e-wallets, investments, and liabilities. Some numbers were easy to notice, while smaller assets were easy to forget. Worthly responds to that problem by helping users build a simple money map from an empty state.

Users can add accounts, liabilities, investments, and transactions, then see an Overview of net worth, cashflow, recent activity, and planning progress. The Assets page separates liquid accounts, investments, and liabilities, while Planning estimates whether the current money map is on track toward a target.

The prototype is local-first, uses dummy data for demonstration, and focuses on clarity, navigation, layout, and accessible financial information.

## Feature Highlights

- Guided setup for building the first money map.
- Overview dashboard with net worth, cashflow, recent transactions, and quick add actions.
- Assets page for accounts, investments, liabilities, and allocation summary.
- Planning page for target-readiness projection.
- History page for income, expense, and transfer records.
- Full-screen add/edit flows for focused financial forms.
- Local JSON persistence with safe dummy data mode.
- Dynamic Type, contrast, readable amount text, accessible labels, and clearer chart colors.

## Design Focus

The main design learning was about layout, navigation, and interaction.

At first, layout felt like placing things in a good position. Through Worthly, layout became more about helping people understand information: what matters first, what should be grouped together, what action is expected, and when a task needs full attention.

The project also became a way to study Apple Human Interface Guidelines more carefully, especially the interaction side of HIG.

## Accessibility Notes

Worthly includes accessibility-focused improvements such as:

- Dynamic Type support for larger text sizes.
- Contrast-aware colors for financial status and chart elements.
- Readable amount text with monospaced digits.
- Accessibility labels for important cards and controls.
- Full-screen editors to reduce distraction during longer forms.
- Touch targets designed around native iOS expectations.

## Project Status

Worthly is still a prototype, not production financial software.

The current version is ready for usability testing with people who manage money across more than one account, wallet, investment, or liability. The next step is to validate whether users understand the money map, the planning result, and the overall flow without extra explanation.
